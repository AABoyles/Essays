John William Perry was a New York City police officer. He had taken leave from his precinct one Tuesday to file his retirement papers. At 38 years old, afer 8 years of service, he was leaving the force to take a job at a Manhattan Law firm (http://www.nypdangels.com/nypd/perry.htm). But on that Tuesday morning, John Perry got word that a plane had slammed into the World Trade Center. So instead of filing his retirement papers, Officer Perry rushed to Ground Zero to help. He was killed when the north tower fell.

Early Life

John William Perry, was born to James and Patricia Perry. He was raised in Seaford, on Long Island. Apparently, he couldn’t tie his shoes or ride a bicycle until he was 9. He spent several early grades in special education classes.

Personal Life

John Perry was an Extropian. He was interested in cryonics, and it is likely1 that he had made the requisite preparations.

September 11

His story was picked up by the New York Times.

Newsday 9-11 Victims’ Database Entry

He was posthumously awarded the New York City Police Department’s Medal of Honor for his heroic actions.
